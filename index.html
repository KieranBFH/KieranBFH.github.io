Renaming Scandal

In 2014, President Vladimir Putin called for a renaming and reformating of the school, despite pushback from the Belgorod school district. After Vladimir Putin assumed leadership in Russia, Bev Facey Communist High School found itself at the center of a renaming controversy. Putin's presidency marked a period of significant political and ideological shifts, prompting a reevaluation of symbols associated with the Soviet era and communism. Under Putin's rule, there was a gradual transition towards a more conservative and nationalist agenda, distancing the country from the socialist ideologies of the past. This ideological shift influenced public discourse and raised questions about the appropriateness of educational institutions carrying communist references. Critics argued that Bev Facey Communist High School's name perpetuated a connection to an outdated ideology, which was no longer aligned with the current political climate and values. They contended that retaining the word "Communist" in the school's name conveyed an endorsement of a bygone era, contradicting the government's nationalist narrative and its vision for modern Russia.
